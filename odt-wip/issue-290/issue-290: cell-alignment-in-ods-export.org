With `ODS` export, the /last/ column header (= header of the /numeric/
column in table below) gets aligned to the ~left~, but the /numeric
cells/ themselves are aligned to the ~right~.  The result is UGLY.

It is understandable that the header gets aligned to the right--it
contains ~text~.  It is understandable that the numeric data in the
cells are aligned to the left--it contains numbers.  When considering
each cell in isolation, this stylistic choice by the ODS exporter is
OK.  But when they /textual header/ and /numeric data/ all appear in a
single column the result is UGLY.

To force a ~right~ alignment for the entire last column, I used the
~:col-cookies~ line as below, but the results are unsatisfactory.  I
consider this a bug.

_*NOTE*_: Even without the ~ATTR_ODT~ line, the default export to
~ODT~ is satisfactory.

#+ATTR_ODT: :col-cookies "| <r> | <l> | <r> |"
#+ATTR_ODT: :rel-width 80
|       Date | Action                  | Satisfaction Level |
|------------+-------------------------+--------------------|
| 2025-03-02 | Performed various tasks |                  1 |
| 2025-03-03 | Baked bread             |                  2 |
| 2025-03-04 | Repaired electric motor |                  3 |
